Glossary

Term Definition
Blackjack Besides being the name of this game, the term Blackjack refers to a two-card, 21-valued hand, consisting of one Ace and one 10-valued card.
Dealer The entity the player competes against.
Double One of the five player decisions. Doubling is only possible as the player's first decision within a round. If the player chooses to double, the wager will be multiplied by two for ths round, after which the player gets to draw exactly one card - no more, no less - and conclude the round automatically hereafter. As the wager is doubled, so is the payout.
Hand The set of cards currently in a player's or the dealer's possession.
Hit One of the five player decisions. If the player chooses to hit, the player will get to draw another card. If this card causes the player's total hand rank to become 21 or more, the round will be concluded automatically.
Player The person playing this game, i.e. you.
Split One of the five player decisions. Splitting is only possible if the player's starting hand consists of two cards of identical rank. If the player chooses to split, will the wager will be increased by the current wager, after which two new hands will be created, each hand consisting of one of the cards in the starting hand, and one newly drawn card.
Stand One of the five player decisions. If the player chooses to stand, the round will be concluded by letting the dealer resolve its hand, after which the player's hand will be compared against the dealer's hand.
Surrender One of the five player decisions. Surrendering is only possible as the player's first decision within a round. If the player chooses to surrender, half of the wager will be recovered, and the round will be ended without play.
